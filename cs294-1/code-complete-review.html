Paper Reviews for Code Complete and Software Project Survival Guide

Feb 4, 2002

 

Code Complete:

Perhaps this book might be useful to a newbie in computer science, but to anyone who knows anything about programming, it’s completely redundant. You might get away with saying that it’s possible to teach good coding style by example, but hitting someone with a lot of rules out of context is exactly the kind of practice that situated cognition researchers have been railing against for 20 years. Unfortunately for educators, it’s also hard to teach good practice because the only software projects that they can give their students are artificial. If you’re the sort of person who likes to be told why a goto can be good and why it might be bad, go ahead and read this book. Otherwise, put it back on the shelf with the other self-help books, and go find a nice novel to read.

 

Software Project Survival Guide

Here’s another winner of a book. After reading the excerpts, if I wasn’t certain before that I never wanted to become a manager, I sure am now. If I ever have to adhere to particular standards and practices that come out of a book or some manual of regulations, I’m going to quit my job and work in the army, where they’ve institutionalized that sort of thing.

Ok, the truth, I find I can’t evaluate this work on its own merits because I have no experience in my life at managing a large software project, so like the paper above, taking these ideas out of context makes me unable to assimilate them. Sorry.
