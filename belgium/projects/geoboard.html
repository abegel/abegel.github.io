Geoboard Teacher's Resource

A good sense of number and relationships between numbers help us make sense of our everyday world. Numbers are used to quantify, identify location and specific objects, in naming and measuring. Developing good number concepts involves active experiences and reflection. The activities in this project offer different ways to explore numbers and their meanings.

Geoboard allows you to experiment with patterns in numbers and view the results on a circular geoboard. The activities with the geoboard lead to ideas of common factors and pre-algebraic expressions.


Grades: 4 to 8
Type: Medium-term

The activities in this "active essay" pose questions which the students solve through experimentation on the geoboard. The problems encourage finding rules in algebra or pre-algebra.


Grades: 9 and up
Type: Long-term

Students can build their own "active essay" on a related mathematical concept. An "active essay" is an HTML document consisting of small MicroWorlds projects which are interactive.
